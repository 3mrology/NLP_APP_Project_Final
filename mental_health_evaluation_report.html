Research Objective

This study evaluates the performance of different AI-powered mental health chatbot systems in accurately detecting and diagnosing a comprehensive range of mental health conditions, including:

  • Major Depressive Disorder & Persistent Depressive Disorder
  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder & Social Anxiety Disorder
  • Bipolar Disorder & Panic Disorder
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder
  • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder
  • Eating Disorders (Anorexia, Bulimia, Binge Eating)
  • Substance Use Disorders
  • ADHD & Sleep Disorders
  • Personality Disorders (Borderline)
  • Psychotic Disorders (Schizophrenia, Schizoaffective)
  • Dissociative Disorders & Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Specific Phobias & Agoraphobia
  • Complex Comorbid Presentations

Key Innovation: TherapeutiGraph Architecture

The primary system evaluated uses a TherapeutiGraph architecture that:

  • Stores patient data, symptoms, behaviors, and disorders as interconnected nodes
  • Enables complex relationship queries for accurate diagnosis
  • Supports real-time pattern recognition across mental health indicators
  • Provides contextual understanding through graph relationships

Post-Processing Enhancement

A sophisticated post-processing algorithm was implemented to refine diagnoses:

def post_process_diagnosis(symptoms, behaviors, current_diagnosis): # Bipolar Disorder Reclassification if current_diagnosis == "Generalized Anxiety Disorder": bipolar_indicators = ["More talkative than usual", "Inflated self-esteem", "Recklessness", "Racing thoughts"] if count_indicators(behaviors, bipolar_indicators) >= 2: return "Bipolar Disorder" # Panic Disorder Reclassification if current_diagnosis == "Generalized Anxiety Disorder": panic_indicators = ["Panic attacks", "Chest pain", "Fear of losing control"] if "Panic attacks" in symptoms or count_indicators(symptoms, panic_indicators) >= 2: return "Panic Disorder" return current_diagnosis

üéØ Conclusions and Future Directions

Key Findings

1. Graph Database Superiority

TherapeutiGraph demonstrated clear advantages in mental health diagnosis, achieving 57% average accuracy compared to 43% for the best competing system.

2. Behavioral Pattern Recognition

Graph-based systems excel at recognizing complex behavioral patterns, particularly for conditions like Bipolar and Panic Disorders that require understanding symptom relationships.

3. Post-Processing Effectiveness

Rule-based post-processing significantly improved diagnostic accuracy, suggesting hybrid approaches (graph + rules) are optimal for mental health applications.

4. Latency vs. Accuracy Trade-off

While TherapeutiGraph has higher latency (14.05s average), this is acceptable for mental health screening where accuracy is prioritized over speed.

Clinical Implications

  • Screening Tool Potential: The system shows promise as a preliminary screening tool, but should complement, not replace, professional diagnosis
  • Pattern Detection: Excellent at identifying subtle behavioral patterns that might be missed in traditional screening
  • Data Integration: Successfully combines multiple assessment scales (PHQ-9, GAD-7) into unified insights
  • Safety Considerations: The system correctly identifies and flags suicidal ideation, crucial for clinical safety

Limitations and Considerations

Study Limitations

  • Sample Size: Limited to available Kaggle datasets
  • Simulated Conversations: Test cases were researcher-created, not real patient interactions
  • Single Language: Evaluation conducted only in English
  • Diagnostic Scope: Limited to four primary mental health conditions

Ethical Considerations

  • Clinical Oversight: System outputs require professional validation
  • Privacy Protection: Sensitive mental health data requires robust security
  • Bias Mitigation: Ongoing monitoring for demographic and cultural biases
